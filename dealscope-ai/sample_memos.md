# DealScope AI Sample Memos

These abbreviated examples show the expected structure and tone. They are not
financial, legal, tax, or investment advice.

## Sample 1: Sparse SaaS Idea

Input:

```text
FreshOps helps small restaurants in Vietnam manage inventory, supplier ordering,
and daily food cost from one mobile dashboard.
```

Expected output pattern:

```text
# DealScope Memo: FreshOps

Confidence level: Low
Input completeness: Sparse
Verdict preview: CONDITIONAL GO

## 1. Executive Snapshot
FreshOps targets a credible operating pain for small restaurants: inventory
leakage, supplier coordination, and food-cost visibility. The idea is worth
validating because the pain is frequent and tied to margin, but current evidence
does not yet prove willingness to pay, workflow adoption, or differentiation
from POS systems, spreadsheets, and chat-based ordering.

Score: 6/10

## 6. Risk Register
| Risk | Severity | Why It Matters | Validation / Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Workflow adoption | High | Restaurants may keep using existing chat and spreadsheet habits | Run pilots measuring weekly active use |
| Low ARPU | Medium | Small restaurants may not support expensive onboarding | Test self-serve onboarding and pricing |
| POS competition | Medium | Existing POS vendors may bundle inventory features | Compare depth and workflow fit |

## 7. Verdict
Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO

The thesis works if FreshOps proves repeated weekly use, clear food-cost savings,
and low-friction adoption inside existing restaurant workflows.
```

## Sample 2: Fintech / Remittance Startup

Input:

```text
RemitBridge is a payroll and remittance platform for Filipino remote workers
employed by US startups. It has 220 beta users and $18K monthly transaction
volume, but no revenue yet.
```

Expected output pattern:

```text
# DealScope Memo: RemitBridge

Confidence level: Medium
Input completeness: Moderate
Verdict preview: CONDITIONAL GO

## Market Intelligence
The pain is credible because remote workers often face cross-border payment
friction, transfer fees, settlement delays, and trust concerns. The market may be
attractive if RemitBridge can identify a narrow wedge where existing providers
are expensive or operationally painful for both workers and employers.

Score: 7/10

## Risk Register
Regulatory and compliance risk should be treated as High. Cross-border payroll,
remittance, KYC, AML, and employer-of-record boundaries can materially affect
cost, launch speed, and product scope.

## Verdict
Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO

The thesis works if the team can prove a compliant wedge, recurring employer
demand, and transaction economics that survive compliance and support costs.
```

## Sample 3: Web3 Credential Startup

Input:

```text
GuildProof is an on-chain credential layer for gaming guilds and contributor
communities. It has 3 pilot communities and 9,000 wallets indexed but no paid
customers.
```

Expected output pattern:

```text
# DealScope Memo: GuildProof

Confidence level: Medium
Input completeness: Moderate
Verdict preview: CONDITIONAL GO / NO-GO

## Competitive Moat
The indexed wallet count is not enough by itself. The key question is whether
communities and game studios treat credential verification as a budgeted
workflow problem or a nice-to-have data layer.

Score: 5/10

## Verdict
Verdict: CONDITIONAL GO

The idea deserves more validation only if pilot communities convert to paid use
or produce a repeatable workflow that cannot be solved by simpler community
management tools.
```
